Extracurriculars and hobbies

Programming

Photo by Fotis Fotopoulos on Unsplash

Programming is the last but certainly not the least of my hobbies. Every day, I spend more than eight hours learning new things. Should I even call this a hobby? I'm not sure if it's enough to label it that way. In 2020, when I started to learn Python, I immediately fell in love with the process of creating something from my mind and defining each step of the process precisely.

Programming has become an art for me. Discovering new, more efficient ways to implement solutions for problems or tasks has deepened my interest in computer science. This interest ultimately led me to change my major from civil engineering to Computer Science. I believe this field is ever-changing, which is why I don't anticipate getting bored with it anytime soon.

Sightseeing and Traveling

Photo by Simon English on Unsplash

I think the world is a beautiful place, and the idea of spending all of my time in one place hurts my soul. There is so much I haven't seen yet on this earth; that is why one of my favorite hobbies is sightseeing. The simple fact of discovering new places and meeting new people makes me happy to be alive. In the future, I hope I can have a lot of stories to tell my kids about my adventures.

Cooking

Photo by Fabrizio Magoni on Unsplash

Some people say that we eat in order to live, not the inverse, but sometimes the delicacy that I create while mixing ingredients makes me wonder about that. The simple act of creating something delicious that brings you happiness just by eating it—isn't that also a joy of life? Most of the time, I try to prepare my meals by myself and be creative about it. Additionally, I like to taste new flavors whenever I can. I think this adds a little bit more color to my life and is therefore also my hobby.

Fitness and Sports

Photo by Alexander Redl on Unsplash

Mens sana in corpore sano, otherwise a healthy mind in a healthy body. I believe this is a great principle for a person's well-being and an efficient way to stay balanced. In recent years, my own discovery of the benefits of fitness and exercise has been very impactful. Being more focused on my work, experiencing less stress than before, and even getting sick less often—these are all positive outcomes from daily exercise.

I usually go jogging to become more resilient, play basketball to improve my focus and become addicted to self-improvement, and sometimes I lift weights to become accustomed to pain. I believe I have gained a well-balanced and healthier life from these activities; therefore, it is not just a hobby but a way of life.
